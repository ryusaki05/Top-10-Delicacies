Adobo: The National Dish of the Philippines

Adobo, the national dish of the Philippines, is a testament to the country’s culinary heritage. It’s a dish that transcends generations, bringing families and communities together around a table filled with its savory and comforting flavors.

The origins of adobo can be traced back to the pre-colonial era. It’s believed that Filipinos, long before the arrival of the Spanish, preserved meat by soaking it in vinegar and salt. This technique, known as “adobo,” proved to be an effective way to extend the shelf life of food, especially in a tropical climate. Over time, the dish evolved with the addition of other ingredients, such as soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns, giving it the distinct flavor profile that we know and love today.

Adobo is a remarkably versatile dish, with variations found across different regions of the Philippines. Some variations include adobong tuyo (dried fish adobo), adobong pusit (squid adobo), and adobong gata (coconut milk adobo). Despite the variations, the core essence of adobo remains the same – a flavorful combination of savory, sour, and salty notes that captures the essence of Filipino cuisine.

Adobo is a dish that transcends culinary boundaries. It’s a dish that’s deeply rooted in Filipino culture, symbolizing unity, tradition, and a sense of belonging. Whether enjoyed with rice, pan de sal (Filipino bread rolls), or simply on its own, adobo continues to be a beloved and iconic dish that embodies the spirit of Filipino gastronomy.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs or drumsticks, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional, for browning)

Procedure

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, water, garlic, peppercorns, bay leaf, and salt. Mix well to coat the chicken.
  2. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight for a more intense flavor.
  3. Heat the olive oil (if using) in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  4. Add the marinated chicken and cook, stirring occasionally, until browned on all sides.

  6. Simmer for 30-45 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly.
  7. Remove from heat and serve hot over rice.
