Igado: A Visayan Delicacy

Igado is tradional Visayan Dish from the philippines that features tender pork liver and belly cooked in a savory and tangy broth. Its origins can be traced back to the indigeneous people of the Visaya region, who were known for their resourcefulness in utilizing readily available ingredients. The dish's distinct flavor profile comes from a combinationof soy sauce, vinegar, and ginger, often with the addition of shrimp paste for a deeper umami depth.

Igado is Commonly served with rice, and it is a popular dish for special occasion and family gatherings. Its rich and flavorful broth, along with the tender texture of the pork, makes it a comforting and satisfying meal. Whether enjoyed as a main course or a side dish, Igado remains a beloved culinary tradition in the Visayas region and beyond.

Igado Recipe

  • 1 lb pork liver, sliced
  • 1/2 cup pork belly, sliced
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup ginger, grated
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup shrimp paste (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercons
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt ( or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions, for garnish

Instruction:

    In a large pot or Dutch oven, combine the pork liver, pork belly,onions, garlic,ginger, soy sauce,vinegar,water,shrimp paste (if using) peppercon and bay leaf. Stir well to combine.
  • Bring the mixture to aboil over medium heat, then reduce heat low, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the pork liver is tender and cooked though. Stir occassionally to prevent sticking.
  • Remove the bay leaf before serving.
  • Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot over rice.
