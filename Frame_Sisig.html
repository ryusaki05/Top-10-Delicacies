Sisig: A Filipino Culinary Icon

Sisig is a beloved Filipino dish that hails from the province Pampanga, known as the "Culinary Capital of the Philippines." It's dish that showcases the boldness and inventiveness of Filipino cuisine, transforminga humble ingredien-pork-into a sizzling and flavorful masterpiece.

The origins of sisig can be traced back to the 1970s, when a food vendor named Aling Luding conceived of a new dish utilizing pig's face parts that were previously discarded. She would boil, grill, and then chop these parts, and then sizzle them on a hot plate with onions, pepper, and a tangy marinade giving birth to the beloved sisig.

Sisig is a sizzling platter ofchopped, grilled, and marinated pork 9usually pig's face ears), combined with onions, bell peppers, and a tangy sauce made of calamansijuice, soy sauce, and chili oil, it's often served on a hot plate, allowing the flavors to meld and the dish to sizzle as you eat. Its distinct aroma and texture, along with the burst of flavors from the marinade, make it a truly unique and addictive dish.

Sisig is typically served with rice and is often enjoyed with a cold beer. Its popularity has extended beyond Pampanga, becoming a staple dish across the Philippines and gaining international recognition.

Sisig Recipe

  • 1 lb pork cheeks or ears, boiled until tender
  • large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice( or lime juice)
  • 1 tablespoon chili oil ( or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt ( or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup chopped spring onions, for garnish

Instruction:

    Boil the pork cheeks or ears in water until tender. Drain and set aside.
  • Once the pork is cool enough to handle chop it into small pieces.
  • Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add the chopped pork, onion,bell peppers, and garlic. Cook, stirring ocassionally, until the vegetables are softened and the pork is slightly crispy, about 10 minutes.
  • In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, calamansi juice, chili oil, black pepper, and salt/ Pour the mixture over the pork and vegetables in the skillet.
  • Cook for a few more minutes, stirring occassionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  • Transfer the sisig to serving platter. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve immediately with rice.
