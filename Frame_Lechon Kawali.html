Lechon Kawali: A Filipino Crispy Pork Delight

Lechon Kawali, a Filipino dish that translates to “fried roasted pork,” is a culinary masterpiece that embodies the Filipino love for crispy and flavorful pork. It's a dish that captures the essence of Filipino celebrations, often gracing tables during special occasions and feasts.

The origins of Lechon Kawali can be traced back to the traditional Filipino method of roasting whole pigs, known as “lechon.” However, Lechon Kawali offers a more convenient and accessible way to enjoy the crispy, savory goodness of pork. It involves marinating pork belly in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and peppercorns, resulting in a flavorful base. The marinated pork is then coated in cornstarch and deep-fried until it achieves a golden brown and incredibly crispy exterior, while the interior remains tender and juicy.

Lechon Kawali is a testament to the Filipino culinary creativity and the art of balancing flavors and textures. Its combination of crispiness, savory richness, and tender meat makes it a truly satisfying dish. Whether served as a main course or a side dish, Lechon Kawali is a beloved Filipino dish that never fails to impress.

Lechon Kawali Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly, skin-on
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying

Procedure

  • In a large bowl, combine the pork belly, soy sauce, vinegar, water, garlic, peppercorns, bay leaf, and salt. Mix well to coat the pork.
  • Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight for a more intense flavor.
  • Remove the pork from the marinade and pat dry with paper towels.
  • In a separate bowl, combine the cornstarch and ¼ cup water. Mix until smooth.
  • Coat the pork belly with the cornstarch mixture, making sure to cover all sides.
  • Heat a large pot or deep fryer filled with vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  • Carefully lower the pork belly into the hot oil. Fry for about 10-15 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.
  • Remove the pork belly from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  • Slice the lechon kawali into thick pieces and serve hot.
