Sinigang: A Filipino Sour Soup Delight

Sinigang is a quintessential Filipino soup dish known for its distinctive sour and savory flavors. It's a true testament to the creativity amd resourcefulness of Filipino cuisine, utilizing a variety of ingredients and techniques to create a truly unique and comforting meal.

The signature sourness of sinigang comes from a variety of sources, most commonly tamarind (tamarind pulp or sinigang mix). Other options include guava, green mangoes< or even tomatoes, each offering a subtle variation in flavor. This sour base is then combined with a broth (typically pork or beef), a medley of vegetables, and sometimes seafood or other proteins.

Sinigang is a staple in Filipino households and is enjoyed year-round. It's a dish that warms the soul on a chilly day, provides a refreshing elements on a hot day, and always offers a comforting and satisfying experience. Whether It's a simple weeknight meal or a special ocassion gathering , sinigang remains a cherished culinary tradition in the Philippines.

Sinigang Recipe

  • 1 lb pork shoulder or beef shanks, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 package (1.75oz) sinigang mix (tamarind, guava, or other)
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 1 cup sliced green beans
  • 1 cup sliced bok choy or chinese cabbage
  • 1/2 cup sliced eggplant (optional)
  • 1/2 cup sliced string beans (optional)
  • 1/2 cup sliced okra (optional)
  • 1/4 cup shrimp paste (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instruction:

  • IN a large pot or Dutch oven, combine the pork, sinigang mix, water, and onion. Bring yo a boil over high heat.
  • Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour, or until the pork is tender.
  • Add the green beans and bok choy. Simmer for 5 minutes, or until vegetables are cooked to your liking.
  • If using , stir in the shrimp paste.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Serve hot over rice.
